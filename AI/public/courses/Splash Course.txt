Module 1: Water Preservation – Because Earth Isn't a Giant Swimming Pool
Water covers ~71% of our planet, but guess what? Only about 0.5% is usable fresh water we can actually drink or use easily. The rest is salty ocean or locked in ice like a cryogenic dinosaur drink.
Did You Know?
* A single leaky faucet dripping once per second wastes ~3,000 gallons a year. That's enough to fill a small hot tub... for your goldfish. ??
* If every U.S. household fixed leaks, we'd save enough water to supply 11 million homes for a year. Imagine the pool parties!
* Taking a 5-minute shower instead of 10 saves ~12.5 gallons per shower. Do that daily and you're basically a water ninja. ??
* An average family of four can save 14,000–25,000 gallons/year just by switching to low-flow toilets. Old toilets flush like they're angry at the world—new ones are chill.
Fun Tip to Save Water (with Humor):
* Shorten your shower to the length of your favorite song. If it's "Bohemian Rhapsody," congrats—you're now a water conservation rockstar. If it's a 30-second TikTok, you're basically Aquaman.
Pro move: Install low-flow showerheads (save 15 gallons per 10-min shower) and turn off the tap while brushing teeth. Your future self (and the planet) will high-five you.
